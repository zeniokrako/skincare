Skin Concerns Guide

Understanding how ingredients interact with 18 skin concerns

Acne Prone Skin

Skin susceptible to developing acne, including blackheads, whiteheads, and inflammatory lesions. Requires careful ingredient selection to avoid pore-clogging substances.

Aging / Fine Lines

Concern about wrinkles, fine lines, loss of elasticity, and other signs of skin aging. Benefits from antioxidants, retinoids, and collagen-supporting ingredients.

Comedogenic

Concern about pore-clogging ingredients. Score indicates comedogenicity: positive values mean more likely to clog pores, negative values mean non-comedogenic or pore-clearing.

Contact Dermatitis

Skin inflammation caused by direct contact with allergens or irritants. Requires identifying and avoiding trigger ingredients.

Dehydrated Skin

Skin lacking water (distinct from dry skin which lacks oil). Can affect any skin type. Benefits from humectants and hydrating ingredients.

Eczema / Atopic Dermatitis

A condition causing dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. The skin barrier is compromised, requiring extra moisturization and gentle ingredients.

Fragrance Sensitivity

Concern about reactions to fragrance compounds and essential oils. Positive score indicates fragrance/sensitizing potential.

Fungal Prone Skin

Skin susceptible to fungal overgrowth (Malassezia). Requires avoiding ingredients that feed fungi, such as certain fatty acids and esters.

Hyperpigmentation

Dark spots, melasma, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, or uneven skin tone. Benefits from brightening ingredients and tyrosinase inhibitors.

Irritation Potential

General tendency to cause stinging, burning, or irritation. Positive score means more irritating, negative means soothing/calming.

Oily Skin

Skin that produces excess sebum, often leading to shine and enlarged pores. Benefits from oil-controlling and non-comedogenic products.

Photosensitivity

Concern about ingredients that increase sun sensitivity. Positive score means the ingredient increases photosensitivity (problematic), negative means it offers photoprotection.

Pregnancy Safety

Concern about ingredient safety during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Negative score means unsafe/avoid, positive means safe/beneficial.

Psoriasis

An autoimmune condition causing rapid skin cell buildup, resulting in scaling and inflammation. Benefits from ingredients that support cell turnover and reduce inflammation.

Rosacea

A chronic inflammatory skin condition causing facial redness, visible blood vessels, and sometimes small bumps. Requires gentle, non-irritating products.

Seborrhoeic Dermatitis

A common skin condition causing scaly patches, red skin, and stubborn dandruff. Often triggered by Malassezia yeast, so avoiding certain oils and fatty acids is important.

Sensitive Skin

Skin that reacts easily to products or environmental factors, often with redness, stinging, or irritation. Requires minimal, gentle formulations.

Very Dry Skin (Xerosis)

Extremely dry skin lacking moisture and natural oils. Requires intensive hydration and occlusive ingredients to restore the skin barrier.
