About This Database

What is Seborrhoeic Dermatitis?

Seborrhoeic dermatitis is a common skin condition causing scaly patches, red skin, and stubborn dandruff. It's often triggered by an overgrowth of Malassezia yeast, which naturally lives on our skin but can cause problems when it grows too much.

Why This Database?

Many skincare ingredients can feed Malassezia yeast, making seb-derm worse. This database helps you identify potentially problematic ingredients so you can make informed choices.

Assessment Methodology

Each ingredient is assessed based on:

  • Chemical structure - Esters, fatty acids (C11-C24), and certain oils are known Malassezia food sources
  • Published research - We prioritize peer-reviewed studies from PubMed
  • Expert sources - Dermatology organizations and recognized experts

Understanding the Indicators

Red - Confirmed to feed Malassezia
Orange - Contains ester bonds (often problematic)
Yellow - Fatty acid or alcohol (check carbon chain)
Purple - Polysorbate (breaks down to fatty acids)
Teal - Toxic to Malassezia (beneficial)
Green - Has antifungal properties
Blue - Generally considered safe
Gray - Not yet researched

Important Disclaimer

This database provides information, not medical advice. Individual reactions vary, and what's problematic for one person may be fine for another. Always:

  • Consult a dermatologist for personalized advice
  • Patch test new products
  • Consider your individual triggers and sensitivities
